Biomedical Robotics RESEARCH

Robotization of a Trans-Oesophageal Echocardiography Probe

Design of a robotic system for remote manipulation of a trans-oesophageal echocardiography (TOE) probe, aiming to improve safety, precision and ergonomics during interventional cardiology procedures.

Medical Robotics Application Field
Remote Control System Approach
Image-Guided Navigation

Project Context

Interventional cardiology enables minimally invasive treatment of complex structural heart diseases. Trans-oesophageal echocardiography (TOE) plays a central role in guiding these procedures in real time.

However, current clinical practice exposes echocardiographers to prolonged X-ray radiation and subjects patients to repeated manual probe manipulations during long procedures.

Project Objectives

  • Design a robotic system for remote control of the TOE probe
  • Reduce clinician exposure to X-ray radiation
  • Improve patient comfort during long interventions
  • Enable precise and repeatable probe movements
  • Integrate image-based guidance for enhanced navigation

Technical Approach

The project focuses on the mechanical and mechatronic design of a robotic interface capable of manipulating the TOE probe with high precision while preserving clinical usability.

The robotic system is combined with image-based feedback, enabling intuitive and accurate control during interventional procedures. Emphasis is placed on safety, ergonomics and seamless integration into the clinical workflow.
