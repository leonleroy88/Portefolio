Biomedical Robotics RESEARCH

Robotization of a Trans-Oesophageal Echocardiography Probe

Research project focused on the development of a robotic assistance system for remote manipulation of a trans-oesophageal echocardiography (TOE) probe. The work addresses safety, ergonomics, and precision challenges in interventional cardiology, while respecting clinical and industrial confidentiality constraints.

Medical Robotics Application Field
Remote Control System Approach
Image-Guided Navigation

Project Context

Trans-oesophageal echocardiography (TOE) is a key imaging modality for guiding minimally invasive structural heart interventions. Its use in interventional settings requires continuous and precise probe manipulation under fluoroscopic guidance.

This project was conducted in a clinical and research environment and involved close interaction with medical professionals. Due to confidentiality considerations, only high-level aspects of the project are presented here.

Project Objectives

  • Explore robotic assistance for remote TOE probe manipulation
  • Address clinician exposure to X-ray radiation
  • Improve ergonomics and patient safety during long procedures
  • Investigate repeatable and stable probe positioning
  • Consider integration with image-based guidance

Technical Approach

The project focuses on the mechanical and mechatronic design of a robotic interface capable of manipulating the TOE probe with high precision while preserving clinical usability.

The robotic system is combined with image-based feedback, enabling intuitive and accurate control during interventional procedures. Emphasis is placed on safety, ergonomics and seamless integration into the clinical workflow.
